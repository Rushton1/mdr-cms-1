    Listed in Recession-Friendly Drink & Eats!, Kitchens Rollin' On Four Dubs!, ANDALE, ANDALE, ANDALE: Give Me My Latin Fix!

    One of the BEST $3 I've ever spent...PERIOD.

    With the numerous Off the Grid events and with so many new food trucks coming into the scene, people often forget about the little lone taco trucks at some random San Francisco street corner. As many of us food truck chasers know, the food can be pricey and is not always what you expect it to be. On many occasions, I know I have been disappointed having wasting my money on tasteless mediocrity. Have no fear, El Gallo Giro Taco Truck will help bring you back, back to the times where you can fill your belly with quick, simple and delicious food with just several dollars in your wallet.

    El Gallo Giro Taco Truck is located in an unassuming part of the Mission in front of a cute little playground. The menu is simple with Tacos ($1.50), Burritos ($5-$7.50), Tortas ($5) and Quesadillas ($6.75) with your choice of meat: Carne Asade (Steak), Al Pastor (Spicy Pork), Lengua (Beef Tongue), Pollo (Chicken), Cabeza (Beef Head) and Camarones (Shrimp).  yelp.com/biz_photos/ot7Q…

    Yesterday, 6.8.11, I needed to grab a quick dinner before heading over to the San Francisco Food Bank to volunteer and thought it would be a perfect time to try the highly rated El Gallo Giro Taco Truck. I ordered Two Lengua Tacos ($3 total) with the cheery and friendly cashier. Before even placing my change into my wallet, I was handed a plate of tacos emanating with vibrant colors saying, "EAT ME NOW!"

    LENGUA TACOS
    yelp.com/biz_photos/ot7Q…
    The double layers of corn tortillas were topped with a heaping portion of thick, succulent chunks of lengua and then sprinkled with onions, cilantro and then a spicy salsa rojo. Almost the whole circumference of the tortillas was covered with lengua perfectly braised to a soft, melt-in-your-mouth tenderness. It had a rich beefy flavor which was brightened with the fresh onions and cilantro. The salsa rojo added a spicy punch and tanginess. The tacos were super fresh, simple, and PACKED WITH FLAVOR! And for just $3, it definitely was a satisfying meal that didn't even scratch the bank.

    El Gallo Giro is just TOO good to pass up or by. Seriously, for once the 5 Star reviews don't lie. EAT HERE NOW!

    One day. Four taco trucks/stands. I don't know why. Maybe this fantastic city/neighborhood provide the opportunity to go to four different taco trucks/stands within a few blocks. Or maybe because I wanted an adventure. Wouldn't be the first time.

    The plan:

    Chicken taco at each location. See what customizing can be done. Sample a bit of each. Rank 'em. Save the rest and mix everything together for a massive burrito for dinner. Give away some as a replate. Post same yelp for all four to give context.

    The verdict:

    1. Tonayense. Average cost at $1.75. Way above average flavor. Black beans were a huge bonus, loved the mild green sauce and oil was minimal. This was easily my favorite.

    2. Mexicali. Second cheapest at $1.50. Tasty. Bonus points for having a bell you ring to get their attention, as well as giving me the opportunity to use a little spanish from 8th grade/Taco Bell to ask for lechuga (lettuce). The friendly lady obliged. More good stuff with the guy who wanted to pay with an ATM card. Didn't work. The lady working and me chipped in to cover his taco. I hope that karma pays off.

    3. Gallo Giro. Incredibly cheap at $1.25. Bit on the spicy side. Oil content seemed standard.

    4. Cachanilla. Most expensive at $2.00. Tasty. Made in the same building but different prep area than Mexicali. I give them credit for being up front saying if I order at the window (Mexicali) it's $1.50, and inside it's $2.00. I paid an extra $0.50 just for the experience.

    I have discovered the platonic ideal of carnitas tacos.

    Of course, being that these are the platonic idea of carnitas tacos, they cost $1.50, and come from a friendly little truck parked outside a playground in the Mission.

    Two are a hearty snack, and they're served properly...double-stacked, warm, corn tortillas, soft but still sturdy, fresh onions and cilantro, salsa that's smoky and warm, but still with a decent amount of heat, and even some radish chunks and pickled vegetables on the side.

    But of course, it's the meat that makes the taco, and this stuff is serious. There's plenty in your pile, and it's a delicious mix of  crispy browned bits and unctuous fatty bits. It's well-seasoned, and aggressively porky as well.

    I also tried the chicharrones, and the chicken tacos, both of which were tasty, but just not in the same league as the carnitas.

    I'll definitely be back. I'd like to pretend I'll try some of the other options, but really I'll probably just keep ordering the carnitas.

    There were so many things that were spot on at the El Gallo Giro Taco Truck: the lady who took our order was very cheery; a bottle of hand sanitizer was thoughtfully placed by the truck; a fold-able table surrounded by four plastic chairs was set up in front of the truck; the prices were satisfactorily low; the menu offered plentiful meat options; our orders were ready before we had a chance to catch our breaths; and the truck was conveniently located outside of a playground where you could sit in the plentiful grass.

    So you've probably guessed it.  My complaint was with the food itself.

    The lengua meat in the tacos were disappointingly tough to chew.  I eagerly anticipated the melt-in-your-mouth lengua that I was accustomed to.  Alas, it could not be found in these tacos.

    The salsa immersed amongst the ingredients was tasty, but did not provide the spicy kick necessary to transform the taco.

    The tacos were smartly wrapped in double-layered tortillas, but the outside tortilla was not crispy.

    The lengua burrito's meat suffered from the same lack of tenderness, but the overall burrito's portion size was filling.

    I will probably make my way to other taco trucks before reconsidering El Gallo Giro.
